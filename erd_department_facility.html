Academic Note on circular FKs: The DEPARTMENT ↔ DOCTOR circular dependency is a classic case. The standard solution is to allow chairman_id to be NULL, insert the DEPARTMENT first (no chairman yet), insert DOCTORs with their department FK, then UPDATE the department row to set the chairman. In SQL DDL this is handled with DEFERRABLE INITIALLY DEFERRED constraints or by inserting with NULL and updating. Always note this in your submission.

Academic Note on multivalued attributes: Per Elmasri & Navathe (the standard textbook), when mapping a multivalued attribute to a relation, the PK of the new relation is the composite of (original entity PK + the attribute value). Surrogate PKs are acceptable in practice but may lose marks in a pure academic ER mapping exercise.
