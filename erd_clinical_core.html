Academic Note on APPOINTMENT: APPOINTMENT maps an M:N relationship between PATIENT and DOCTOR with extra attributes (fee, payment_status, refund_date). This is the correct pattern — you cannot put these attributes on either PATIENT or DOCTOR directly, so they live on the relationship/bridge table. The surrogate PK (appointment_id) is acceptable and preferred over a composite PK here because cancelled appointments may be re-scheduled (same patient+doctor, different time).

Academic Note on ROOM_RESERVATION (Ternary): A ternary relationship links three entities simultaneously. In Mermaid crow's foot notation there is no direct ternary notation, so it is modelled as an associative entity with three FK lines — this is the standard approach and is academically correct.
